Research

Research Experience

  1. Molecular Dynamics Simulation of Alloying Characteristics of Al-Mg Nanoparticles under different process heating conditions. (Published)

    Molecular Dynamics, Coalescence Kinetics of Bimetallic Alloy

    Research work under the supervision of Dr. Mohammad Nasim Hasan. The study investigates the effect of thermal process parameters on the alloying of the Al-10Mg (wt%) system using molecular dynamics. Five different heating rates were applied to Al and Mg nanoparticles (NPs) to observe melting, coalescence, and subsequent cooling. The research focused on alloying temperature, atomic migration, coalescence kinetics, and mechanical properties at various heating rates. Initial simulations determined the melting temperatures and thermo-stability of single particles (SPs) of Al and Mg. Results indicated that heating rates significantly affect the melting temperature of NPs. The alloying process included heating, relaxation, and cooling/solidification phases, followed by uniaxial tensile tests to assess mechanical behavior. While heating rates and coalescence kinetics showed a substantial relationship, they had minimal impact on mechanical properties.


    Read the preprint: [Molecular Simulation GMOS]

  2. Effect of powder bed fusion process parameters on microstructural and mechanical properties of random sized High/Medium Entropy Alloy (2023 - Current)

    Additive Manufacturing, Powder bed Fusion

    Working with Dr. Mohammad Nasim Hasan on the field of Additive Manufacturing we propose a molecular dynamics simulation of the powder bed fusion process, involving multiple randomly-sized particles distributed on a solid copper bed following a Weibull distribution. Each particle is modeled with a core-shell structure, coated with another material. The system is subjected to heating via a LASER beam to simulate the additive manufacturing process, with a focus on varying LASER parameters such as speed, direction, and power. After heating, the system will be cooled to room temperature, and mechanical tests will be conducted on the resolidified alloy. This study aims to investigate the formation of voids within the resolidified alloy and assess their impact on the alloy's mechanical properties.

  3. Numerical analysis of wind load on cross-section of rectangular and staggered high rise buildings:A Case Study (Undergrad Thesis)

    Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD)

    This study examines the effect of wind load on high-rise buildings by analyzing drag and lift coefficients using ANSYS Fluent fluid dynamics simulations. The research aims to optimize building arrangements and orientations to minimize wind-induced forces, enhancing urban architecture's resilience. Using Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), simulations were conducted on rectangular slender cylinders in various configurations and air angles of attack (AOA). A housing project in Uttara, Dhaka, consisting of six staggered high-rise buildings, was analyzed to determine drag and lift coefficients at different wind directions. Results show that drag and lift coefficients are minimized at AOAs of 25-35 degrees, reducing pressure forces. In staggered configurations, upstream buildings face higher drag forces, which decrease with increased longitudinal spacing. Higher aspect ratios also reduce drag due to delayed vortex formation. These findings offer valuable insights for designing safer, wind-resistant high-rise buildings.
